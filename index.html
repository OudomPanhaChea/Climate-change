What is Climate Change?

Before and after climate change. (Image credit: Aithor)

Climate change refers to significant, long-term changes in the global climate. These changes affect temperatures, weather patterns, and ecosystems. It can occur naturally, because of the changes in the sun's activities or major volcanic eruptions.

However, according to the United Nations (2023), since the 19th century, human actions have been the primary cause of climate change, not only because of the burning of fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and gas but also deforestation and industrial processes. These activities release large amounts of greenhouse gases, particularly carbon dioxide (CO2), which trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to global warming (United Nations, 2023).

The consequences of climate change are vast and include rising global temperatures, melting polar ice caps, sea-level rise, and more frequent extreme weather events such as hurricanes, droughts, and floods (United Nations, 2023). Climate change not only threatens the natural world but also human health, food security, water supply and economic stability.
