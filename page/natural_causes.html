Natural causes of climate change

A portion of global warming can be linked to natural events. Throughout the history of our planet, volcanic eruptions, variations in the amount of solar energy received, and minor changes in the way our planet moves around the sun have all resulted in noticeable impacts on the patterns of warming and cooling on Earth (Turrentine, 2022).

However, climate data can demonstrate that the current increase in global temperatures is occurring much faster than ever before. Moreover, according to Turrentine (2022), even though these natural causes are still active, their impact is too minor to account for the rapid warming observed in the past few decades.

Human causes of climate change

The primary cause of climate change is the increase in greenhouse gases in the Earth's atmosphere as these gases trap heat from the sun, creating a "greenhouse effect.". Human activities, particularly the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas, release large amounts of carbon dioxide (CO2) and methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O). As stated by the United States Environmental Protection Agency (2024), the levels of carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide in the Earth's atmosphere have reached higher levels than at any point in the past 800,000 years. The rise in greenhouse gas emissions has intensified the greenhouse effect, leading to an increase in the Earth's surface temperature.
