Mitigation and Adaptation

The differences of mitigation and adaptation of climate change. (Image credit: Tree-Nation)

Mitigating climate change

Mitigation refers to the effort of preventing or reducing the emission of greenhouse gases (GHG) into the atmosphere to make the impacts of climate change less severe. One of the most effective ways to mitigate climate change is by transitioning to renewable energy sources, such as wind, solar, and hydropower (World Wildlife Fund, 2022). These sources generate electricity without producing greenhouse gases and can significantly reduce the worldâ€™s reliance on fossil fuels. In addition, we must stop deforestation and bring back our natural environments until we achieve net-zero carbon emissions, which means that the amount of greenhouse gases being released into the air should be equal to the amount being absorbed and stored in areas such as the roots of trees (World Wildlife Fund, 2022).

Adaptation climate change

Adaptation involves preparing for the negative impacts of climate change and taking the necessary steps to avoid or reduce the harm they might lead to, or seizing the opportunities that may arise. According to the World Wildlife Fund (2022), the initial action in adapting to climate change is recognizing the specific dangers in your area and creating strategies to handle them. Adaptation strategies include developing drought-resistant crops and changing planting seasons to match new climate conditions. In urban areas, adaptation efforts focus on building infrastructure that can withstand extreme weather events, such as flood barriers, and heat-resistant buildings (World Wildlife Fund, 2022). Also, making sure to manage food and water properly to prepare for extreme weather events.
